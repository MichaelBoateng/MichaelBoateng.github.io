I am a Ph.D. student in Electrical and Computer Engineering at Georgia Tech, which is ranked amongst the best engineering school in the U.S. I work as a Graduate Research Assistant in the Energy Division of the AI Institute for Advances in Optimization (AI4OPT), where I apply artificial intelligence and optimization techniques to enhance power grid reliability and efficiency.

My research focuses on developing novel machine learning approaches for Security-Constrained AC Optimal Power Flow (AC SCOPF), AC power flow feasibility restoration, and Volt/VAR optimization in large-scale transmission networks. I am supervised by Prof. Pascal Van Hentenryck and Prof. Daniel Molzahn at Georgia Tech, with additional mentorship from Dr. Russell Bent at Los Alamos National Laboratory (LANL) and Prof. Parikshit Pareek at IIT Roorkee.

Currently, I serve as a Graduate Research Consultant at the Electric Reliability Council of Texas (ERCOT), where I collaborate with engineers on machine learning solutions for contingency analysis using real-world operational data from the Texas grid. I also hold a Graduate Research Intern position at LANL, where I design end-to-end learning frameworks focused on embedding AC non-linearities to market clearing problems.
